# QueerNomads Product Repositioning

## Updated Product Vision
QueerNomads is evolving from a story-sharing community app into a **city-intelligence-first platform for queer digital nomads**. The product should help people make better travel and relocation decisions by combining structured city insights with queer community context.

**Positioning statement:**
> QueerNomads helps queer digital nomads discover, compare, and understand cities through both data and community-informed context.

This direction intentionally avoids becoming a generic ranking clone. The differentiator is the combination of:
- practical decision support (cost, safety, internet, weather, lifestyle fit)
- queer-specific relevance
- transparent methodology and clear confidence labeling
- warm, inclusive editorial voice

## Target Users
### Primary users
- Queer remote workers and freelancers choosing their next destination.
- Queer professionals evaluating short-list cities for 1-6 month stays.
- Queer travelers considering medium-term relocation and needing practical confidence checks.

### Secondary users
- Allies and partners planning travel with queer friends or family.
- Early contributors who share lived experience context for cities.

## User Problems to Solve
1. **Decision overload:** Too many destination choices and fragmented data sources.
2. **Safety uncertainty:** Difficulty understanding queer safety nuances from generic travel rankings.
3. **Lifestyle mismatch risk:** Cities can look great on paper but fail personal fit expectations.
4. **Trust concerns:** Existing rankings often hide methodology and overstate certainty.
5. **Context gap:** Hard to blend objective metrics with community-informed signals.

## Core Product Pillars
1. **Identity and belonging**
   - Explicitly queer-focused framing and respectful language.
   - Inclusive defaults (pronouns, varied identities, non-assumptive copy).
2. **City intelligence**
   - Structured city cards and detailed score dimensions.
   - Comparative discovery tools that are quick to scan and reason about.
3. **Decision support**
   - Search, filter, sort, and shortlist-oriented UX.
   - City detail narratives that explain trade-offs, not just totals.
4. **Trust and clarity**
   - Published methodology, limitations, and update recency.
   - Honest labels for heuristic and seed/mock data.
5. **Portfolio-grade execution**
   - Polished design system, coherent page architecture, maintainable code.

## V1 Scope
### In scope
- Repositioned homepage around city intelligence and queer-informed context.
- Shared layout improvements (header/footer/navigation/tone consistency).
- Typed city data model and structured seed dataset.
- City discovery/ranking view with search, filter, sort, and empty states.
- City detail page with score breakdowns, best-for tags, pros/cons, and related cities.
- Methodology/trust content explaining score construction and limitations.

### Out of scope for V1
- Full social network features (follows, feeds, DMs, comments, reactions).
- Heavy auth/permissions redesign.
- CMS or admin panel complexity.
- Advanced geospatial/map stack.
- AI recommendation system.

## Non-Goals
- Becoming a broad “all nomads” ranking website.
- Presenting synthetic scores as objective truth.
- Shipping large feature volume at the cost of coherence.
- Rewriting the stack without user-facing value.

## How City Intelligence Supports QueerNomads Identity
City intelligence is not replacing community identity; it is the delivery mechanism for it. QueerNomads should answer practical planning questions while preserving mission and warmth:
- Scores are framed as **decision aids**, not definitive verdicts.
- City pages include concise queer context notes and safety framing.
- Language remains empathetic and credible, avoiding fear or hype.
- Data and community context are presented side by side where possible.

## Trust and Tone Principles
- **Be explicit:** If data is seeded/mock or heuristic, state it clearly.
- **Be bounded:** Avoid absolute claims in sensitive categories like safety.
- **Be transparent:** Explain calculation logic and confidence level.
- **Be respectful:** Use inclusive language and avoid stereotyping locations.
- **Be useful:** Every metric should map to a real planning decision.

## Design Direction
The UI should feel premium, calm, and trustworthy with an editorial-product blend:
- Elevated but restrained visual hierarchy.
- Consistent spacing, typography, and component rhythm.
- Warm brand expression (subtle rainbow identity accents, not visual noise).
- Accessible contrast and readable long-form content.
- Practical interactions focused on comparison and clarity.
